Overview

At my previous co-op position with Impossible Metals, one of my main projects was this hydraulic end effector system. The main objective of this system was to replace a previous actuation system that controlled the claw during nodule pick testing.

⏬Scroll below to learn more!

Tools:

Solidworks
3D Printing
Machinary & Tools

Utlizing the previous test data and needed parameters for the end effectors, calculations were completed to convert a linear force to pressure within a hydraulic cylinder. With a given general schematic, I chose to sketch out necessary component to get an overview and to visualize mounting the new system onto the machine.

Simultaneously, components such as tubing, cylinders, adapters, valves, etc. were sourced to fit the expected values from the calculations. For the components to adapt onto the test machine, mounts were designed in Solidworks to then be 3D printed for use. Below, the black part, is an example.

I then mounted and assembled everything. Communicated with the electrical technician for circuit connection to the driver to get the motor moving for system to operate. This test apparatus was built rapidly within 1 month to fit in the testing timeline needed for the robot development.
